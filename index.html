About Milky Mushroom (Calocybe indica)

Milky Mushroom, also known as Calocybe indica, is a tropical edible mushroom cultivated widely in India. Known for its thick, fleshy texture and pure white color, it is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients and health benefits.

Health Benefits

  • Boosts immunity with bioactive compounds and antioxidants.
  • Supports heart health by reducing cholesterol levels.
  • Rich in fiber, aiding digestion and improving gut health.
  • Contains anti-cancer properties due to polysaccharides and beta-glucans.
  • Good for managing diabetes by regulating blood sugar levels.
  • Strengthens bones with calcium and vitamin D.

Nutritional Value (per 100g fresh mushroom)

  • Energy: ~30–35 kcal
  • Protein: 3–4 g
  • Carbohydrates: 4–5 g
  • Dietary Fiber: 1–2 g
  • Fat: < 1 g (low-fat food)
  • Vitamins: B-complex, Vitamin D
  • Minerals: Potassium, Phosphorus, Calcium, Iron

Milky Mushroom Gravy (Curry)

A rich and creamy curry with a luscious gravy made from tomato puree and cashew paste. This restaurant-style dish is perfect for serving with naan, roti, or steamed rice.

Ingredients:

  • Milky mushrooms – 200 g (chopped)
  • Onion – 2 (sliced)
  • Tomato – 2 (pureed)
  • Ginger–garlic paste – 1 tsp
  • Green chili – 1
  • Cashew nuts – 8 (soaked & ground to paste)
  • Red chili powder – 1 tsp
  • Turmeric – ¼ tsp
  • Garam masala – ½ tsp
  • Coriander powder – 1 tsp
  • Fresh cream or milk – 2 tbsp (optional)
  • Oil/butter – 2 tbsp
  • Salt – to taste

Procedure:

  1. Heat oil/butter, sauté onions till golden.
  2. Add ginger–garlic paste and green chili → fry.
  3. Add tomato puree and cook till oil separates.
  4. Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
  5. Add mushrooms, stir well, cover and cook 5 minutes.
  6. Add cashew paste and little water to make gravy.
  7. Simmer for 5 minutes → finish with garam masala and cream/milk.
  8. Garnish with coriander and serve with rice/naan.

Milky Mushroom Fry (Dry Curry)

A simple and spicy stir-fry where mushrooms are cooked with onions, tomatoes, and aromatic spices. It's a quick dish that pairs wonderfully with flatbreads or as a side to a main meal.

Ingredients:

  • Milky mushrooms – 200 g (sliced)
  • Onion – 1 (finely chopped)
  • Tomato – 1 (chopped)
  • Green chili – 2 (slit)
  • Ginger–garlic paste – 1 tsp
  • Red chili powder – 1 tsp
  • Turmeric – ¼ tsp
  • Coriander powder – 1 tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Oil – 2 tbsp
  • Curry leaves & coriander leaves – few

Procedure:

  1. Clean mushrooms well and cut into thin slices.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add curry leaves, onion, and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn golden.
  3. Add ginger–garlic paste → fry for 1 minute.
  4. Add tomatoes → cook till soft.
  5. Add turmeric, chili, coriander powder, and salt.
  6. Add mushrooms, mix well, cover, and cook for 8–10 minutes on medium flame (no need of water, mushroom releases moisture).
  7. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve with roti or rice.

Milky Mushroom Biryani

A fragrant and aromatic one-pot rice dish. Layers of spiced milky mushrooms and fluffy basmati rice are slow-cooked to perfection, creating a royal and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • Basmati rice – 1 cup
  • Milky mushrooms – 200 g (sliced)
  • Onion – 2 (sliced)
  • Tomato – 1 (chopped)
  • Ginger–garlic paste – 1 tsp
  • Green chili – 2
  • Mint & coriander leaves – ½ cup each
  • Curd – ½ cup
  • Biryani masala – 1 tbsp
  • Turmeric – ¼ tsp
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves) – few
  • Oil/ghee – 3 tbsp
  • Salt – to taste

Procedure:

  1. Wash and soak rice 30 min → cook till 70% done → drain.
  2. Heat oil/ghee, fry whole spices, onions till golden.
  3. Add ginger–garlic paste, green chili, tomato, and fry.
  4. Add curd, biryani masala, turmeric, and salt.
  5. Add sliced mushrooms, cook 5 minutes.
  6. Layer cooked rice on top, sprinkle mint & coriander.
  7. Cover with tight lid → cook on low flame (“dum”) for 15 min.
  8. Serve hot with raita.

Milky Mushroom Pakoda

A crispy and addictive snack. Mushroom strips are coated in a spiced gram flour batter and deep-fried until golden. Perfect for a rainy day, served hot with tea or chutney.

Ingredients:

  • Milky mushrooms – 150 g (cut into strips)
  • Gram flour (besan) – 1 cup
  • Rice flour – 2 tbsp
  • Red chili powder – 1 tsp
  • Carom seeds (ajwain) – ½ tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Oil – for frying

Procedure:

  1. Mix besan, rice flour, chili powder, ajwain, and salt with little water to make thick batter.
  2. Dip mushroom strips in batter.
  3. Heat oil → deep fry till golden and crisp.
  4. Serve hot with chutney/tea.

Milky Mushroom Soup

A light, comforting, and healthy soup. Finely chopped mushrooms are simmered in a flavorful broth, creating a warm and nourishing appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • Milky mushrooms – 150 g (finely chopped)
  • Onion – 1 (chopped)
  • Garlic – 3 cloves (chopped)
  • Butter – 1 tbsp
  • Corn flour – 1 tbsp (mixed in 2 tbsp water)
  • Black pepper – ½ tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Fresh coriander – few sprigs
  • Water/veg stock – 3 cups

Procedure:

  1. Heat butter in a pan → sauté garlic and onion till soft.
  2. Add mushrooms and cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Add 3 cups of water/veg stock → boil 10 minutes.
  4. Blend half portion (optional) for creamy texture.
  5. Add corn flour slurry, boil till soup thickens.
  6. Season with salt & pepper → garnish with coriander.
